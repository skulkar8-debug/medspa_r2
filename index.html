Dataset Architecture & Methodology
Every metric in this document is produced by a live intelligence pipeline, not a static dataset.
The analysis that follows is generated from STOC's Corporate Development Support (CDS) intelligence layer. Each target in the dataset is scored, classified, and refreshed continuously across 20+ acquisition signals. What follows defines what that means in practice.
STOC's target universe of 17,383 locations is broader than the American Med Spa Association's enumeration of approximately 10,488 licensed medical spas (AmSpa, 2024 State of the Industry). The difference is intentional. STOC maps all U.S. locations offering medical-grade aesthetic services under physician supervision, including standalone medical spas, cosmetic dermatology practices with aesthetic service lines, and plastic surgery clinics with non-surgical revenue. This broader scope reflects how PE platforms actually build acquisitions routinely include adjacent practice types that operate economically as med spas regardless of licensure classification. Wherever comparisons to AmSpa data are made in this document, the scope differential is disclosed.

The Pre-Consolidation Window
The 0% bucket is not simply undiscovered territory. These are markets with sufficient target density, verified supply gaps, and no incumbent PE pressure. Entry economics in a 0% CBSA are structurally different from a 6-15% CBSA: seller expectations are lower, multiples are uncompressed, and de novo remains a viable complement to acquisition.
First-Mover Implication
In 22% of tracked CBSAs, PE entry has begun compressing multiples. CDS tracks competitive arrival in real time across all 686 markets, flagging which 0% CBSAs are receiving inbound sponsor interest before a transaction closes.
